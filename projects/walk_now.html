Overview

I competed this project over the course of 1 weekend for a COVID-19 focused hackathon, hack:now. My teammates and I created an iOS app to simulate the process of taking a walk, specifically around our UC Berkeley college campus. The app reads in the number of steps you have taken today in real life from your Apple Health app, and allows you to take the corresponding number of steps in Google Street View mode. Users can trade in steps for donations to organizations fighting COVID-19. In addition to coding, I took on the task of designing the UI for the application.

Inspiration

Given the COVID-19 situation, many of us are unable to go outside and enjoy a walk like we normally would. We are limited to our immediate neighborhood and unable to explore new places; we also miss familiar places such as our college campuses. Many people also tend to be physically inactive while sheltering in place, with very low step counts.

Design Inspiration

The overall vibe that I wanted the app to convey was one of positivity through letting users visit familiar locations in new ways. I chose gold as the primary color for the design since gold/yellow conveys happiness and warmth. I used icons to make the application seem more friendly and approachable. I included an inspirational quote about love at the bottom of the “My Steps” page to further give the user a positive outlook on life.
