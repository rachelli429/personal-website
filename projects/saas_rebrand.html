Overview

As the new Internal Vice President of the Student Association for Applied Statistics (SAAS), I had creative control over all things related to design. I decided to lead a rebranding initiative for the following reasons:

1. Our old assets and designs were inconsistent

When myself and other SAAS members created designs over the past year, we realized that there was little guidance on what colors and fonts to use. We chose whichever colors and fonts best fit our specific purpose, as long as the colors were blue and pink. This resulted in inconsistency across designs, so we wanted to standardize our branding visuals to better help designers.

2. The focus of the club has shifted

Up until fall 2019, we were affiliated with the Statistics Department at UC Berkeley. In the summer of 2019, we split from the Statistics Department due to differences in vision for the organization. As a department-affiliated organization, we previously had to ensure that we retained a focus on statistics. Since our split, the club has shifted to focus more on applications of statistics and expand our projects to include fields such as econometrics, natural language processing, and business analytics.

3. Emergence of new clubs

Over the past few years, more data science and tech clubs have emerged on campus. We wanted to create a clear, unique brand in order to better distinguish ourselves from these clubs and convey our value and culture to potential members and clients.

Logo

Overall, we liked our old logo but felt that it would benefit from a few small fixes.

We wanted to keep the normal distribution because it is recognizable and a symbol that people clearly associate with statistics.

Our new logo features a gradient from pink to blue that is purple in the middle, bolder text, and a slight change in the shape of the normal distribution so that the bars in the middle are taller. We also created versions of the logo that had our clubâ€™s full name.

Colors

We decided to keep pink and blue as our primary colors. Pink connotes feminity, gentleness, and compassion, which enhances our message of inclusivity. Blue connotes peace and trust, which emphasizes our goal of helping members build confidence.

We turned the saturation down on the old pink and blue, deciding on #FFCOCB for the pink and #87CEEB for the blue.

We chose purple #C0C2E2 as our secondary color, since it would be used often in gradients.

Design Elements

Gradients

We offer a wide spectrum of opportunities for our members across 9 different committees. Our members come from different backgrounds and are interested in different areas within statistics. Gradients help to capture all of this diversity.

Waves

Our use of waves represents how we connect club members within SAAS to each other as well as to faculty, companies, and other campus organizations.
